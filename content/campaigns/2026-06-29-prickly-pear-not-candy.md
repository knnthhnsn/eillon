# Campaign: Prickly pear, but not candy.

**Experiment:** EXP-006  
**Loop:** social_to_letter  
**Automation:** weekly_social_to_letter  
**Date:** 2026-06-29  
**Status:** draft — do not publish without human review

## Hypothesis

If we publish a quiet sensory social kit reframing prickly pear as mineral and skin-close (not sweet), then niche fragrance seekers on Pinterest, Instagram, and TikTok will subscribe to The Letter for studio notes and Beles restock windows, because the objection “prickly pear = candy” is common and EILLON’s Beles chapter answers it with precise note language.

## Campaign angle

**Brand fit:** 3/3 — memory-led, sensory, anti-DTC; directly tied to Beles · Fico d'India note structure.

Prickly pear in perfume is often assumed sugary. Beles holds the fruit against cactus water, pear skin, hibiscus, and mineral air — airy, watery, close to skin. This kit invites curiosity without promising availability or restock dates.

## Primary landing

**The Letter** — `/about` (newsletter form, `product_slug=all`)

**Secondary:** `/beles` for restock list (`#waitlist`)

## Core hooks (platform-agnostic)

1. Prickly pear, but not candy. Mineral air, pear skin, warm stone.
2. Fico d'India — sun-warmed fruit held against cactus water.
3. A green-pink parfum that wears close. Not loud. Not sweet.
4. Watery prickly pear, then hibiscus, then stone on skin.
5. If you skip fruit scents for being too sugary — read the note list first.
6. Beles · Fico d'India. Oil-rich. Composed in Copenhagen.
7. Cactus fruit without the syrup. Mineral drydown, soft musk.
8. Studio notes on prickly pear — sent slowly, one letter at a time.
