# Campaign: Prickly pear, but not candy.

**EXP-ID:** EXP-006  
**Date:** 2026-06-29  
**Loop:** social_to_letter  
**Automation:** weekly_social_to_letter  
**Angle:** Objection-aware prickly pear positioning — fruit without syrup, mineral air instead of candy sweetness.

## Hypothesis

If we draft a social kit framing Beles as prickly pear without candy sweetness, then niche fragrance seekers on Pinterest, Instagram, and TikTok will click through to Beles restock or The Letter, because sensory copy matches skin-scent and “not too sweet” search language.

## Primary landing

**Beles restock:** `https://eillon.maison/beles#waitlist`  
**CTA:** Receive the Beles restock note before the public boutique opens.

## Secondary landing

**The Letter:** `https://eillon.maison/about#letter`  
**CTA:** Studio notes, sent slowly — restock windows and chapter openings.

## Core hooks (platform-agnostic)

Use as feed captions, pin descriptions, or reel supers. Lowercase-friendly; no hashtag storms.

1. Prickly pear, but not candy. Mineral air instead.
2. Sun-warm fruit held against stone — not syrup.
3. Fico d'India: green-pink, watery, close to skin.
4. The pear note you wanted — without the sugar rush.
5. Oil-rich parfum that wears close. Pear skin, cactus water, warm stone.
6. A prickly pear chapter composed in Copenhagen — airy, not sweet.
7. Skin scent logic: pear at the wrist, mineral at the collarbone.
8. Beles · Fico d'India — join the restock list before the boutique opens.
9. Studio notes on chapters like Beles — sent slowly.
10. Memory perfume: desert fruit at noon, not dessert.
