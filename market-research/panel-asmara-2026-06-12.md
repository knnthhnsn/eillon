# Synthetic Panel Report — Asmara Eau de Parfum (2026-06-12)

## Decision question
Which size/price configuration drives more purchase intent for Asmara in the European
market: **€240 / 100 ml** (Cell A) or **€170 / 50 ml** (Cell B)? Secondary: where is
demand concentrated, and what blocks conversion?

## Concept tested
Asmara — Eau de Parfum by Elli Hansen (independent house, Copenhagen). Petrichor
fragrance: rainwater/bergamot/green stems → wet stone/violet leaf/orris → vetiver/
patchouli/soft musk. Quiet, mineral, unisex. Sculpted oval flacon, ivory cap, cloth
case. Sold direct online + Copenhagen studio. 2 ml sample available at €28 (shown to
both cells).

## Panel
40 personas, identical across both cells (within-subjects, zero carryover — each cell
elicited independently). Stratified: age 18–24 ×5, 25–34 ×13, 35–44 ×9, 45–54 ×7,
55+ ×6 · 22 F / 16 M / 2 NB · income 7 low / 20 mid / 13 high · engagement 6
heavy-niche / 15 moderate / 13 light / 3 lapsed / 3 non-user. Realism quota: 21/40
personas with weak engagement, tight budgets, or loyalty elsewhere.

## Headline results

| Metric | Cell A — €240/100ml | Cell B — €170/50ml |
|---|---|---|
| Mean purchase intent (1–5) | **2.42** | **2.42** |
| Top-2-box (full panel) | 17.2% | 17.4% |
| Top-2-box (target segment*) | **32.1%** | **32.0%** |
| Distribution 1→5 | 23 / 33 / 27 / 14 / 3 % | 23 / 33 / 27 / 14 / 3 % |

*Target segment = heavy/moderate engagement with mid/high income (n=20 per cell).

**There is no demand cliff between €240/100ml and €170/50ml.** The two configurations
are statistically indistinguishable overall and within every major segment. Buyers in
the target segment treat both as "normal niche pricing"; non-buyers are out of the
market at either price. Per-persona deltas are small and offsetting: the lower entry
price helped a few cash-flow-constrained considerers (content creator +0.50, gift
buyer +0.40), while a few value-oriented enthusiasts preferred the 100 ml's per-ml
math (collector journalist −0.67, pediatrician −0.75).

Flagged gaps (>0.5): engagement heavy vs light (+1.4), income high vs low (+1.2),
age 25–34 vs 18–24 (+1.1). The buyer is an engaged, affluent 25–54 niche customer;
Gen-Z and low-income personas admire the brand but are out of the market entirely.

## Why — qualitative themes

**Drivers**
1. **Composition credibility.** Enthusiasts read violet leaf/orris/vetiver as a real
   structure, not marketing: "Finally, someone attempting petrichor seriously" (P04).
2. **Anti-ubiquity.** The strongest intent came from people fleeing Sauvage/Byredo
   saturation: an unknown Copenhagen house is the point (P17, P34, P39, P23).
3. **The object.** The flacon/cloth case consistently landed — design-led buyers and
   content creators called it shelf- and camera-worthy (P15, P23, P31, P27).

**Objections**
1. **Cannot smell before buying** — the single dominant objection, voiced across
   nearly every segment including personas for whom price was "not the issue" (P02,
   P09, P14, P18, P26, P29). No counters, no stockists, mail-order sample only.
2. **€28 sample reads as a toll.** Called "steep", "cheeky", "mad", "offensive" —
   even by personas who said they'd pay it. Several computed €14/ml and balked.
   Note: the site FAQ hints at a sample credit; **no persona knew about it** because
   the product story doesn't surface it.
3. **Longevity doubt.** "Quiet mineral = gone in two hours" appeared in 9+ verbatims
   among the most likely buyers (P06, P08, P20, P26, P30, P34, P37, P39). The page
   gives them no wear-time evidence to counter it.
4. **No reviews/track record** — Fragrantica-literate buyers won't move before
   third-party validation exists (P08, P21, P24).

**Price perception:** within the target segment, both prices read as fair-to-modest
for niche ("almost suspiciously reasonable next to Roja" — P34). Outside the target
segment, unaffordable at any size. Price is not the conversion lever.

**Representative voices**
> "The price isn't the issue; the process is." — P02, consultant, Zurich (A)
> "I'll just go and smell it, no risk... If it has presence, I'm buying it, possibly that same afternoon." — P23, brand director, Copenhagen (A)
> "I'd do the €28 sample without thinking twice, and if the drydown holds up at my desk past lunch, I'd buy the bottle." — P17, PE analyst, London (B)
> "I won't pay Ormonde Jayne money for forty minutes of damp pavement." — P30, professor, Edinburgh (A)
> "The sample alone costs more than I spend on lunch in a week... For my budget this is a non-event." — P24, PhD student, Heidelberg (B)
> "I'd skip the sample and just buy the 50 ml... If it's good, I'm six months ahead of everyone." — P39, founder, Tallinn (B)

## Recommendation
Keep the current ladder — the €240/100ml hero does not suppress demand relative to
€170/50ml, so there is no case for leading with the smaller bottle. The conversion
lever is the **trial funnel**, not the bottle price: (1) explicitly credit the €28
sample against a full-bottle purchase and say so everywhere the sample appears —
this converts the single most-resented number on the page into a deposit; (2) add
concrete wear-time/longevity language to the product story to neutralize the
"quiet = vanishes" fear held by the likeliest buyers; (3) seed third-party validation
(Fragrantica listing, review samples to fragrance communities), since the engaged
segment explicitly waits for it. Demand is real but bottlenecked: ~32% top-2-box in
the target segment is a strong concept signal; the panel says they're waiting at the
sample stage, not the price tag.

## Methodology & limits
Synthetic panel using Semantic Similarity Rating (PyMC Labs / Colgate-Palmolive
2025, arXiv:2510.08338): LLM personas gave free-text reactions which were scored
against Likert anchor statements in a separate blind pass. The method reaches
~90% of human test-retest reliability on survey purchase intent — it estimates
what people would *say* in a survey, not what they will buy. Scores are most
trustworthy as comparisons (between prices, variants, segments), in categories
the model knows well. Validate against real sales/survey data where available.
