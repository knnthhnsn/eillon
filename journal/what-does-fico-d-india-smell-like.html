What does Fico d'India smell like?

EILLON studio · 29 June 2026

Fico d'India smells like sun-warmed prickly pear — watery pulp, green stem, and pear skin held against mineral desert air. It is brighter than berry, softer than citrus, and closer to skin than loud projection. In Beles · Fico d'India, that fruit accord opens the parfum before hibiscus and cactus bloom fold in, then soft musk and warm stone settle for hours.

The opening — fruit and water

At first contact, prickly pear reads airy and sun-warm: cactus water and pear skin with a faint green edge from the stem. The fruit is irregular — sweetness earned by heat, not candy. That is the character we name on the bottle and build the chapter around.

The heart — blossom without powder

After the fruit opening settles, hibiscus and cactus bloom arrive — floral, but dry and sun-lit rather than powdery. Green leaves keep the accord lifted. In an oil-rich parfum base, this heart typically becomes clearer within the first hours of wear.

The dry-down — mineral and close

Soft musk, mineral air, and warm stone hold the trail close to wrist and collarbone. Beles is composed as a skin scent: an oil-rich parfum that unfolds slowly, not an alcohol-forward spray built to fill a room.

What it is not

Fico d'India in Beles is not a gourmand prickly pear, not a loud fruity cocktail, and not a guaranteed all-day projector. It is genderless, niche, and hand-finished in Copenhagen — fruit placed against stone and air. How to read it on skin

Apply once — wrist or collarbone — and wait. Oil-rich parfums reward patience. Do not rub. You will read the blossom heart after the fruit opening; the mineral musk trail follows in the hours after. Fragrance care guide →
