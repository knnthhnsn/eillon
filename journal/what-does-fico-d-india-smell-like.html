What does Fico d'India smell like?

EILLON studio · 29 June 2026

Fico d'India is Italian for prickly pear — the fruit accord at the centre of Beles · Fico d'India. On skin it reads as sun-warmed fruit with green stem and watery pulp: brighter than berry, softer than citrus, and closer to the body than a room-filling spray.

The opening — fruit and water

The first minutes are prickly pear, cactus water, and pear skin — a bright, slightly green lift that feels like heat on cactus pad rather than candy. There is sweetness, but it is earned by sun, not sugar. If you search for a prickly pear parfum that is not gourmand, this is the intent behind the opening.

The heart — bloom on warm stone

As the fruit accord settles, hibiscus and cactus bloom come forward — floral, but dry and sun-warmed, without powder or heavy projection. In an oil-rich parfum base the heart typically reads clearer after the first hour, often within three hours of wear if you apply once and wait.

The dry-down — mineral air close to skin

Soft musk, mineral air, and warm stone hold the trail. Beles is composed to stay near skin — a quiet skin scent for readers who prefer parfum that unfolds slowly rather than announcing from across a room. What is a skin scent? → · Fragrance care guide →

How it differs from a typical fruit perfume

  • Not candy-sweet — fruit placed against mineral air
  • Oil-rich parfum — unfolds over hours, not minutes
  • Genderless — composed for skin, not a demographic label
  • Copenhagen studio — hand-finished, niche scale

How to smell it properly

Apply once to wrist or collarbone. Do not rub. Oil-rich parfums reward patience — the blossom heart opens as the fruit accord dries down. After six hours you will read the mineral musk trail most clearly.
