What does Fico d'India smell like?

EILLON studio · 29 June 2026

Fico d'India smells like sun-warmed prickly pear — watery fruit pulp, green cactus stem, and a mineral dryness that keeps the accord from turning candy-sweet. Brighter than berry, softer than citrus, with a close-wearing warmth that sits on skin rather than filling a room. In Beles · Fico d'India, that fruit accord opens with prickly pear, cactus water, and pear skin, then folds into hibiscus and cactus bloom before soft musk and mineral air settle in the dry-down.

The fruit accord

Prickly pear pulp is irregular — guarded by spines, sweetness earned by heat. In perfumery the note reads as green-pink fruit with a watery edge: cactus water at the open, pear skin adding a thin waxiness, desert green from the pad itself. It is not a polite citrus sparkle or a familiar berry gourmand.

What it is not

Fico d'India is not built as a sugary fruit bomb. EILLON places the accord against mineral air and warm stone so the parfum feels airy and sun-warm — closer to skin scent than to loud projection. If you expect tropical punch or vanilla-heavy sweetness, this accord will read restrained instead.

How it unfolds on skin

Beles is composed as an oil-rich parfum — a high load of perfume oils designed to ripen slowly. Apply once to wrist or collarbone and wait; do not rub. The blossom-soft heart typically opens within the first hours; the mineral musk trail follows as the fruit accord settles. Wear is close — a trace you notice when you move, not a cloud that announces you across a room.

Questions

Is Fico d'India the same as prickly pear?
Yes — Fico d'India is Italian for prickly pear. The name on the Beles bottle describes the central fruit accord.
Is this a sweet perfume?
No — brightness is removed from candy and placed against mineral air and warm stone.
