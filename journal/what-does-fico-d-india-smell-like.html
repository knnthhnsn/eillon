What does Fico d'India smell like?

EILLON studio · 29 June 2026

Fico d'India smells like sun-warmed prickly pear held against mineral air. At first you read cactus water and pear skin — bright, watery, with a green stem note that keeps the fruit from turning sugary. The heart opens into hibiscus and cactus bloom; the dry-down settles into soft musk and warm stone, worn close to skin rather than projected across a room. That is the accord we named on the Beles bottle.

The opening — fruit and water

Prickly pear pulp is irregular: guarded by spines, sweetness earned by heat. In perfumery it reads brighter than berry and softer than citrus — pulp with green stem still attached. We open Beles with prickly pear, cactus water, and pear skin so the first minutes feel airy and sun-warm, not candied.

The heart — blossom without powder

After the fruit settles, hibiscus and cactus bloom appear — floral, but dry and sun-lit, folded with green leaves. In an oil-rich parfum base the blossom reads clearer once the watery top has time to breathe, typically within the first hours of wear.

The dry-down — skin and stone

Soft musk, mineral air, and warm stone hold the accord close. Beles is composed as a parfum — a high load of perfume oils — so the trail unfolds slowly on wrist or collarbone. It is genderless, niche, and hand-finished in Copenhagen.

What it is not

Fico d'India in Beles is not a gourmand prickly pear, not a loud room-filler, and not a guaranteed all-day projection. It is a quiet skin scent built from memory and place — the fruit accord at the centre of our first chapter.
