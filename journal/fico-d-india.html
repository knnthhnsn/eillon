What Fico d'India means in Beles

EILLON studio · 16 June 2026

Fico d'India is Italian for prickly pear — the paddle cactus fruit that turns sun-drenched and honeyed when ripe. On the Beles bottle the name sits beneath Beles, naming both the fragrance and its central accord: warm fruit, green stem, and desert air held in an oil-rich parfum base.

Why prickly pear

Prickly pear is not a polite citrus or a familiar berry. It is irregular, guarded, and slow — pulp protected by spines, sweetness earned by heat. That tension fits how we compose at EILLON: fruit that feels sun-warmed rather than sugary, floral without powder, warmth without heaviness.

In Beles · Fico d'India the fruit opens bright — prickly pear, cactus water, pear skin — then folds into hibiscus and cactus bloom before soft musk and mineral air settle close to skin. Prickly pear parfum — a quiet guide → · What Fico d'India smells like →

Oil-rich, not alcohol-forward

Beles is built as a parfum: a high load of perfume oils in the composition, designed to unfold over hours on skin. It is not a diluted eau de toilette built for loud projection and fast fade. The Fico d'India accord needs an oil base to ripen — the floral heart reads clearer after the fruit opening settles, typically within the first three hours of wear.

How to test it

Apply once to skin — wrist or collarbone — and wait. Oil-rich parfums reward patience. Do not rub. After three hours you will read the blossom-soft heart; after six, the mineral musk trail.

On the bottle

Fico d'India appears in quiet serif on the square flacon, above the winged leopard emblem. It is the link between the Italian name for the fruit and the Danish studio that composed the parfum.
