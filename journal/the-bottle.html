The Object

The bottle as architecture

EILLON studio · 16 June 2026

The Beles · Fico d'India flacon is cut from a single block — a flat square in opaque matte glass with softened corners. It is not a classic oval eau de cologne silhouette. It reads as object first, fragrance second: quiet, weighted, meant to stay on a shelf like sculpture.

The cap

A tall rectangular stopper in brushed silver, proportioned to the square face and weighted to close without fuss. No dome, no ornament — just precise geometry and cool metal against warm glass.

The face

One unbroken panel. No label wrap, no champagne band. EILLON, Beles, and Fico d'India are set in restrained serif directly into the surface — the same hierarchy you read on the bottle in hand.

The emblem

At the base, a winged leopard rests in fine stipple, draped in classical cloth. It is the bottle's only figurative gesture — mythic, still, deliberately small against the empty matte field above. The emblem appears in motion on the site; on the flacon it is permanent ink.

Made for parfum

Beles is an oil-rich parfum, not a thin alcohol-forward spray. The flacon is sized and weighted for a concentrate you apply with intention — each bottle hand-finished in Copenhagen and wrapped in stone-tone cloth and cream archival paper.
